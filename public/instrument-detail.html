Violin

Historical Context

The violin is a string instrument, usually with four strings tuned in perfect fifths. It is the smallest and highest-pitched instrument in the string family, and it is played with a bow. The modern violin was developed in the 16th century in Italy and has since become a key instrument in classical, jazz, and folk music.
